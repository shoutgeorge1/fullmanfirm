What Happens If You're Being Sued by Creditors in California?

If you are being sued by a creditor, it is critical to act quickly. If you ignore any legal papers sent to you by a credit card company or bank, the court will issue a default judgment. This means you are legally responsible for the debt, and the creditor can:

  • Freeze your bank accounts (bank levy)
  • Garnish your wages
  • Place a lien on your property
  • Damage your credit score

At The Fullman Firm, we provide legal defense against creditor lawsuits. Our experienced credit defense attorneys will:

  • Prepare a legal response to the lawsuit
  • Demand that the creditor provide evidence the debt is actually yours
  • Identify procedural errors that can lead to case dismissal
  • Defend your rights under state and federal consumer protection laws
  • Work to get your case dismissed, not settled

We help you explore all your legal options so you can make the best decisions about defending your case. Our goal is to get cases dismissed by finding procedural errors and violations of consumer protection laws.
